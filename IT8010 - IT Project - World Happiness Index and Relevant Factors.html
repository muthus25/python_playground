Abstract:¶

Objective

The aim of the study is to identify and investigate if there is a correlation between the happiness score of countries and factors such as Gender Gap, Linguistic Diversity and Scholastic Performance of Students.

Methods

In order to carry out the research, we make use of web scraping with Python and retrieve appropriate indices for each of our variables from official or other reliable data sources from the web. With the help of Python Pandas and Seaborn libraries, we visually represent the data in scatter plots with regression lines and interpret the results. To further confirm our outcome, significance tests are conducted by utilizing Python Scipy library and arriving at a Pearson Correlation Coefficient for the relationships.

Results

Statistically significant moderate positive linear relationship has been found between Happiness Score and Scholastic Performance of students as measured by the PISA score. Likewise, a statistically significant weak negative linear relationship is found between Happiness Score and Linguistic Diversity. On the other hand, no linear relationship is observed between Happiness Score and Gender Gap and this correlation seems statistically insignificant.

Conclusion

The findings indicate that there is a moderate positive linear relationship between Happiness Score of countries and the Scholastic Performance of Students, a weak negative correlation between Happiness Score and Linguistic Diversity and no correlation between Happiness Score and Gender Gap.

Happiness Score of Countries¶

United Nations Sustainable Development Solutions Network publishes the World Happiness Report every year, based on the resolution adopted by UN General Assembly in July 2011, in order to help guide public policy (World happiness report 2020, 2020). World Happiness Report use data that come from the Gallup World Poll surveys. These surveys ask main life evaluation questions to the respondents by requesting them to think of a ladder and rate their lives on a scale of 0 to 10 (where 0 is the worst possible life and 10 is the best possible life). The rankings and scores of countries are derived from the survey scores with the use of Gallup weights, from nationally representative samples. The levels of GDP, life expectancy, generosity, social support, freedom, and corruption - are estimated to contribute to make life evaluations higher. Typically, the annual sample of each country is 1000 people. The sample size would be 3000 for countries which had surveys each year. This sample size is large enough to provide a good estimate at the national level. These are confirmed by 95% confidence levels.

Index: World Happiness Index 2020

Organization Responsible: United Nations Sustainable Development Solutions Network

Official Website: https://worldhappiness.report/

Original Data Source: World Happiness Report 2020

Gender Gap¶

First introduced by the World Economic Forum in 2006, the Global Gender Gap Index is a framework to capture the scale of gender-based disparities and the progress that happens over years (The global gender gap index 2020, 2020). The index benchmarks national gender gaps on economic, education, health and political criteria, and provides country rankings that allow for effective comparisons across regions and income groups. The quantitative analysis and methodology behind these rankings serve as a basis for planning measures that could effectively reduce gender gaps. The procedure to determine index scores is designed in such a way as to count situations in which men are disadvantaged relative to women as "equal". This report covers as many countries as possible and as of 2020 this includes data from 153 countries out of which 107 have been consistently included from the first edition of this publication.

Index: Global Gender Gap Index 2020

Organization Responsible: The World Economic Forum

Official Website: https://reports.weforum.org/global-gender-gap-report-2020/the-global-gender-gap-index-2020/

Original Data Source: Global Gender Gap Report 2020

Data Scraped from: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Gender_Gap_Report

Linguistic Diversity¶

Greenberg's Diversity Index, also known as the Linguistic Diversity Index, measures the language diversity in various countries. It is the probability that two randomly selected people from the population will have different mother tongues (Linguistic diversity index.2021). While a 0 indicates that everyone has the same mother tongue, a 1 indicates no two people have the same mother tongue.

Scholastic Performance of Students¶

PISA stands for Programme for International Student Assessment conducted by the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development. It measures the ability of 15-year-old students across various countries to use their reading, mathematics and science knowledge and skills to meet real-life challenges. The knowledge and skills of students are measured through an internationally agreed upon metric. For the 2018 PISA study, a two-stage sampling procedure was conducted (Programme for international student assessment - PISA.). At first a representative sample of at least 150 schools were selected, taking into account factors such as location (state or province; but also whether the school is located in a rural area, town or city) and level of education. In the next stage, close to 42 15-year-old students were randomly selected from each school to take the assessment. Most countries assessed between 4000 and 8000 students. Students selected to take the PISA assessment received sampling weights so as to represent the entire PISA-eligible cohort. It is, therefore, considered to be the most reliable and comprehensive indicator of students capability across nations and it serves as a tool for countries to understand and fine-tune their educational policies. Hence, this score can be used as an indicator of Scholastic Performance of Students in a country for the purpose of our research.

Index: Programme for International Student Assessment (PISA) 2018 Score (Most recent)

Interpreting Scatter Plots:¶

A scatterplot displays a graphical relationship between two sets of data. Each dot in a scatter plot represents a single data point. A regression line (a straight line) is a line of best fit. If the line slopes upward as we move from left to right, this will specify a positive relationship between X and Y. As the X-values increase, the Y-values tend to increase. On the other hand, if the line slopes downward as we move from left to right, this indicates a negative relationship between X and Y (Deborah J, 2016). In this case, as the X-values increase, the Y-values tend to decrease. If the line is horizontal, then there exists no relationship between X and Y. It may be worthy to note how close the data points are to the line of best fit. If all or most of the data points lie on the line of best fit, the correlation is expected to be strong. But if most of the points lie above or below the line, the correlation is said to be weak as there are many outliers to the line.

Interpreting Pearson Correlation Coefficient:¶

Pearson Correlation coefficient is a helpful way to examine the direction and strength of linear relationships between two variables.

Direction & Strength: The direction of the relationship can be interpreted by the sign of the relationship. If the coefficient is positive, there is a positive correlation between the two variables and if the coefficient is negative, there is a negative correlation between the variables (Interpret the key results for correlation.). The correlation coefficient can range in value from 1 to +1. The larger the absolute value of the coefficient, the stronger the relationship between the variables.

Statistical Significance: The p-value should be compared to the significance level (alpha level) we have decided. The alpha level we use for this study is 0.05 which works well for most case. The p-value tells us whether the correlation coefficient is significantly different from 0.

P-value : The correlation is statistically significant

If the p-value is less than or equal to the significance level, then we can conclude that the correlation is different from 0.

P-value > : The correlation is not statistically significant

If the p-value is greater than the significance level, then we cannot conclude that the correlation is different from 0.

Discussion:¶

This study demonstrates a correlation between happiness score and two of the three variables we studied, namely Scholastic Performance of Students and Linguistic Diversity. While there is a moderate positive correlation between Scholastic Performance of Students and Happiness Score, there is a weak negative correlation between Linguistic Diversity and Happiness Score. These results provide insights on how factors such as these impact the happiness of people and these should be taken into account when the government makes decisions on variouis policies.

However, there are some limitations to this study. It focuses only on linear relationship between variables. Future studies on this topic could analyse and establish if there is a causal relationship between these variables. One other limiation is that the data taken into consideration do not include all the countries across the globe. Moreover, the variables that were investigated are of interest to the researcher and does not have any other significance as the literature lacks information on what kind of social and economic variables that are needed to be studied. Further researches could also be done to identify the variables that are common to the happiest countries and perform independent study on the correlation between those variables and happiness index.

Conclusion:¶

Although GDP might be thought of as the best indicator of a nations happiness by some, the World Happiness Report has shown that it need not necessarily be true. It is undeniable that GDP is a good indicator of the standard of living of a society and it has some influence on the happiness of people in a country but it is not all that matters. There are various social factors that play a key role in the happiness of people across countries. It is interesting to note that there is a moderate/weak correlation between happiness and factors such as Linguistic Diversity and Scholastic Performance of Students. Studying more relevant variables could help policy makers in understanding the factors that influence the well-being of people in their country.
